Personas Assignment UIUX

Assignment Introduction and Summary

This assignment, for CSCI 1300 at Brown University, instructed us to find, observe, and interview people using a public interface. Once we had gathered observations and responses to our questions, we were to create two personas based on our findings that represented two different types of users that would be using our targeted interface. We also were instructed to create a storyboard for one of our personas to illustrate their interaction in more detail.

I chose a shared microwave in my apartment building as my interface. The microwave was generally pretty standard, although it had a lot of buttons and features on its control panel. My research identified two broad personas among the users: one persona that wanted to get their food cooked with little effort, and another who valued cooking their food well. The interface generally worked well for the first type of user, since they could start the microwave with just one click, but the second type of user found the more advanced functionality unclear and hard to use.

Interface

  • This interface is trying to allow users to cook food quickly and efficiently, to whatever specification they desire.
  • The fastest way to start the microwave is by just clicking one of the numbers 1-5, which activates the express cook and runs the microwave for the specified number of minutes.
  • To set the cook time manually, the user must press the cook time button and then input the desired number of minutes and seconds on the keypad, at which point they press start.
  • The user can start the timer for 30 seconds or add 30 seconds to the cook time by pressing the "Add 30 Sec" button, and can stop the cooking by pressing Cancel or by opening the door
  • Setting the power level can only be done while the microwave is running, or while the cook time mode is selected
  • Trying to set the power level at the wrong time causes a lower-pitched beep and leads the display to slowly cycle through the message "Power level may not be changed at this time"

Research

Questions

  1. What types of food do you normally cook in the microwave?
  2. How long do normally you cook your food for?
  3. What features on the microwave have you used?
  4. Are you more focused on getting things heated quickly, or getting things to exactly the right temperature?
  5. Do you find that it is easy to do what you want on the interface?

Responses

  • Respondents used the microwave in varying ways: 2 respondents saying they mostly reheated leftovers or prepared ingredients for cooking (water, butter, frozen ingredients), while the third said they mostly cooked pre-made frozen meals.
  • One respondent had grown up in Europe and primarily used microwaves with a knob to set the timer rather than a keypad, and did not feel as familiar with the interface as the others did.
  • One respondent was confused by the extra buttons and features and wished for a simpler interface with just the timer and power level.
  • While most respondents just used the Express Cook settings and didnâ€™t particularly care if their food came out a little overcooked, one respondent was more particular with timings, aiming to cook their food to the perfect heat level.
  • The more particular respondent found setting the timer beyond the express settings to be unintuitive, and found changing the microwave power level to be difficult as well.

Observations

  • Many users just used quick time select or add 30 seconds
  • One user was trying to melt butter for 20 seconds, but was frustrated since they initially tried to click the 2 button, which added 2 minutes to the timer.
  • Users seemed confused by the multitude of extra functions with uncertain functionality; one user tried to use the defrost function and found the menu unintuitive, while another struggled to set the power level

Personas

Busy Beth

Thinks

  • I just want to get this cooked
  • Why are there so many buttons?
  • I guess it started!
  • This is a bit overcooked, but that's fine

Feels

  • Glad the microwave started immediately
  • Confused at the extra functionality
  • Distracted, doesn't want to think about cooking
  • Wants to set microwave and come back to it done

Says

  • Will this button start it?
  • Oh, that worked!
  • I'm going to check my phone while it cooks
  • This is a bit too hot, oh well

Does

  • Immediately presses Express button
  • Goes off to do other tasks while food is cooking

Description

Busy Beth primarily reheats leftovers and wants to get her food cooked as with as little effort as possible, even if there is imperfect heating.

She does not face many interface problems and is able to get what she wants done quickly by using the Express Cook feature, although she finds all the extra buttons a bit overwhelming.

2 of the users I interviewed were like Beth in that they just wanted their food heated quickly, so they just the Express Cook feature with little customization.

Precise Peter

Thinks

  • Why is this not working?
  • Almost warm enough, maybe a little more
  • It looks like the food is done, I'll take it off now
  • Maybe lower power will cook this better

Feels

  • Determined to get food temperature right
  • Frustrated at complexity of setting his preferences
  • Annoyed at the slow power level error message

Says

  • How do you set the cook time?
  • Why is the power level not working?
  • A little too cold, maybe 20 more seconds

Does

  • Tries to press number on keypad without realizing it is Express Cook
  • Presses power level without setting timer first
  • Takes food out and puts back in multiple times
  • Watches food while it cooks

Description

Precise Peter is a perfectionist who doesn't like his food overcooked. He prefers spending a few seconds to set the timer manually and likes to adjust the power levels to get the perfect heat level.

Peter was frustrated, since he initially tried to set the timer without clicking the "Cook Time" button, which ran the Express Cook by default and automatically started the microwave on full power. He also found that setting the power level was confusing, since the button wouldn't work unless the timer was set.

Like Peter, one of my respondents was more precise with their cooking and had similar problems with the interface.

Storyboard (Precise Peter)

1. Peter is hungry and grabs some leftovers from the fridge

2. He goes to the microwave and puts the leftovers in, aiming to get the food cooked to the perfect temperature

3. He tries to set the power level, in order to ensure more even cooking, but is met with an error message

4. He then tries to set the timer for 1:45, but the express cook triggers and the food immediately starts cooking for 1 minute instead

5. He cancels the cooking, and notices the cook time button, pressing that instead

6. He enters his cooking time and tries the power level button again

7. This time, he can set the power, so he puts the microwave at half power and presses start

8. After it finishes, he takes the food out and tries some

9. The food is a bit too cold, so he puts it in for another minute, using the same process as before

10. The food is now perfectly cooked! Peter sits down to eat it
